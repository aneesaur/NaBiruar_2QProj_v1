NoughtHungry: For Zero Hunger

NoughtHungry: For Zero Hunger is a Philippines-based organization advocating for food accessibility through spreading awareness with credible information and sharing valuable resources and opportunities related to our goal. Promoting Sustainable Development Goal 2: Zero Hunger, our group aims to make an impact in eliminating food insecurity in our country and in the world. In the Philippines, we take our own steps in contributing to the national cause and around the world, we promote, support, and collaborate with organizations that share our values. The struggle against hunger is not for one individual or even one community, but for the entire globe. Together, we strive to make change and move forward to a better world for everyone.

What is hunger?

As defined by the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations, hunger is “an uncomfortable or painful physical sensation caused by insufficient consumption of dietary energy.” Hunger can cause a dangerous variety of repercussions, such as malnutrition and death. Each day, millions of people are at risk of facing the consequences of hunger for several different reasons.

Key Information

Global Impact

According to a global statistics report by the World Health Organization in 2024, a number of between 713 to 757 million people were undernourished in 2023, with the preceding two years having an alarming, high count of such as well. The Global Hunger Index as of 2024 indicates a score of 18.3, based on 127 countries, with 42 at hunger ratings of Serious, Alarming, and Extremely Alarming. Progress toward Zero Hunger has not only become stagnant, but has also slightly regressed.

National Impact

In the Philippines, hunger has been a persisting major issue for a long period of time. A report in March 2025 presented survey-based facts that 27.2% of Filipino families or 7.5 million households were recently faced with hunger (Philippine Daily Inquirer, 2025), not counting the actual number of individuals affected. The Philippines has been consistently ranked among the top most food insecure countries in Southeast Asia (Steventon, 2023).

Taken Initiatives

To deal with the problem of hunger, a number of organizations have been established as global support. The World Food Programme is dedicated to food provision, delivering emergency relief to hunger-afflicted countries, and takes part in the development of agriculture. The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations is a specialized agency under the United Nations, similar to the World Food Programme, that works toward food security globally. The list of institutions that are for the same cause goes on and on, all with their own unique systems and services and all looking forward to a future free of hunger.

Significance of Understanding

It is important to understand hunger because it is not just the issue of one person or one country, but of the world. Hunger is caused by a multitude of factors, a number of which we created and continue to create for ourselves. Therefore, we must all take responsibility for our actions and do what we can to promote food security and eradicate hunger.

Sustainable Development Goal 2: Zero Hunger

In 2015, the United Nations established the 17 Sustainable Development Goals, to be integrated with the 2030 Agenda that aims for global peace and prosperity. Among them, Sustainable Development Goal 2: Zero Hunger stands as the appeal to end world hunger. As we move closer to 2030, Zero Hunger is unfortunately still far away from truly being reached, but let us not lose hope. Let this be a reminder of what we are fighting for, a hard push toward the future that we want to build for the world.

There are those who can eat three complete meals each day and there are those who can only come by scraps. Both are human. Both want to survive; both want to eat.

A meal is best shared. Eat well. Eat together.
